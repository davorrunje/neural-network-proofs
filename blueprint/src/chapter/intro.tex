\chapter{Introduction}

This blueprint presents three formalized universal approximation theorems (UATs) for neural
networks, developed in Lean~4 and Mathlib. Each result is stated informally, accompanied by its
formal Lean statement and a prose proof, and is cross-linked to the formal API. Every item marked
green is fully formalized and \texttt{sorry}-free.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Cybenko (1989)}: a single-hidden-layer network with a continuous sigmoidal
    activation is dense in $\Ck$.
  \item \textbf{Leshno--Lin--Pinkus--Schocken (1993)}: an $M$-class activation densely
    approximates iff it is not (a.e.) a polynomial.
  \item \textbf{Monotone networks}: depth-4 monotone networks are universal for monotone functions
    (Mikulincer--Reichman 2022; Sartor et al.\ 2025).
\end{itemize}
